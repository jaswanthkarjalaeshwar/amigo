Great question! Let me explain how I mapped the 20 questions to MBTI personality types:

MBTI Scoring System Breakdown
The MBTI has 4 dimensions, each with 2 opposing preferences:

1. E (Extraversion) vs I (Introversion) - Energy Direction
Questions 1, 5, 9, 13, 17: Focus on social energy and interaction preferences
Scoring: Option 'a' = +1 to E, Option 'b' = +1 to I
Examples:
"UB City Mall with friends" (E) vs "Reading at Cubbon Park alone" (I)
"Mingle with lots of people" (E) vs "Deep conversations with few friends" (I)
2. S (Sensing) vs N (Intuition) - Information Processing
Questions 2, 6, 10, 14, 18: How you gather and process information
Scoring: Option 'a' = +1 to N, Option 'b' = +1 to S
Examples:
"Big picture and brainstorm ideas" (N) vs "Practical details and execution" (S)
"Theory and concepts first" (N) vs "Learning through experience" (S)
3. T (Thinking) vs F (Feeling) - Decision Making
Questions 3, 7, 11, 15, 19: How you make decisions
Scoring: Option 'a' = +1 to F, Option 'b' = +1 to T
Examples:
"How it affects everyone involved" (F) vs "Logical pros and cons" (T)
"Help person improve and grow" (F) vs "What needs to be corrected" (T)
4. J (Judging) vs P (Perceiving) - Lifestyle Approach
Questions 4, 8, 12, 16, 20: How you approach the outside world
Scoring: Option 'a' = +1 to J, Option 'b' = +1 to P
Examples:
"Structured plan for your day" (J) vs "Keep options open and spontaneous" (P)
"Start early, make a plan" (J) vs "Work in bursts of inspiration" (P)
Final Type Calculation
After all 20 questions:

Count scores for each letter (E vs I, S vs N, T vs F, J vs P)
Determine dominant preference in each dimension (higher score wins)
Combine the 4 letters to get the final type
Example: If someone scores E=12, I=8, S=7, N=13, T=15, F=5, J=11, P=9

Result: ENTJ (E > I, N > S, T > F, J > P)
Why This Works
Balanced questions: 5 questions per dimension ensures comprehensive coverage
Real scenarios: Bangalore-specific situations make it relatable and authentic
Clear distinctions: Each option clearly represents one preference over another
Weighted equally: Each question has equal weight (1 point) for fair assessment
